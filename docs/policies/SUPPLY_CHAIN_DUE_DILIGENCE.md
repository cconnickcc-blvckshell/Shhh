## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how [COMPANY_NAME] assesses and manages modern slavery and human trafficking risks in our supply chain, in support of our [Modern Slavery Statement](MODERN_SLAVERY_STATEMENT.md) and [Human Trafficking Policy](HUMAN_TRAFFICKING_POLICY.md).

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to suppliers of goods and services, including:

- Technology and cloud providers
- Payment processors
- Marketing and analytics
- Professional services (legal, accounting, etc.)

---

## 3. Risk Assessment

### 3.1 Risk Factors

- **Sector:** Higher risk in sectors with known labour issues (e.g. manufacturing, agriculture)
- **Geography:** Operations in jurisdictions with weaker labour protections
- **Size and complexity:** Larger, more complex supply chains may have higher risk

### 3.2 Our Supply Chain

Our primary suppliers are technology and service providers in developed markets. We prioritise suppliers with strong ethical and labour practices.

---

## 4. Due Diligence Process

### 4.1 Onboarding

- Request supplier to confirm compliance with our [Supplier Code of Conduct](SUPPLIER_CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
- Review supplier policies and practices where relevant
- Include contract terms requiring compliance with labour and human rights standards

### 4.2 Ongoing

- Periodic review of suppliers (e.g. annually for high-risk or critical suppliers)
- Monitor for adverse news or regulatory findings
- Request updates on policies and practices if risk changes

### 4.3 Red Flags

- Evidence of forced labour, child labour, or trafficking
- Refusal to provide information or cooperate
- Adverse media or regulatory findings
- Audit findings

---

## 5. Escalation

If risks are identified:

- Assess severity and urgency
- Engage with supplier to address
- Consider suspension or termination if remediation is not possible
- Report to Legal and Compliance
- Document actions taken

---

## 6. Documentation

- Maintain records of supplier assessments and due diligence actions
- Support annual Modern Slavery Statement reporting
