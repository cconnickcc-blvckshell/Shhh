# Conflict of Interest Policy

**Effective Date:** [EFFECTIVE_DATE]  
**Classification:** Internal  
**Audience:** Employees, Contractors, Directors  
**Owner:** Legal / HR

---

## 1. Purpose

[COMPANY_NAME] requires that team members avoid conflicts of interest that could compromise their judgment or the company's interests. This policy defines conflicts and the process for disclosure and management.

---

## 2. Definition

A conflict of interest exists when a person's personal, financial, or other interests could reasonably be seen to influence their duties or decisions on behalf of the company.

---

## 3. Examples

- **Financial:** Ownership in a competitor, supplier, or customer; outside employment that competes with the company
- **Personal:** Family or close personal relationships with business partners, employees, or users in a way that affects decisions
- **Gifts and hospitality:** Accepting gifts or hospitality that could influence judgment
- **Use of position:** Using company resources, information, or position for personal gain

---

## 4. Disclosure

- Disclose potential conflicts to your manager or [CONFLICT_CONTACT_EMAIL] as soon as you become aware
- Directors and senior leaders must disclose conflicts to the board or equivalent
- Annual disclosure may be required

---

## 5. Management

- Disclosed conflicts are reviewed and managed (e.g. recusal, divestment, or other measures)
- The company will not unreasonably restrict outside activities, but may require steps to address conflicts

---

## 6. Failure to Disclose

Failure to disclose a known conflict may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
