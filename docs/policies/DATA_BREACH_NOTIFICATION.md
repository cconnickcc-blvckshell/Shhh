## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines when and how [COMPANY_NAME] notifies individuals and regulators of a personal data breach, in compliance with GDPR Art. 33–34, CCPA, and other applicable laws.

---

## 2. When to Notify

### 2.1 Regulators (GDPR Art. 33)

- **When:** Without undue delay and, where feasible, within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach. The clock starts when we have a reasonable degree of certainty that a personal data breach has occurred. If we cannot provide full details within 72 hours, we may provide initial notification and follow up in phases.
- **Who:** Supervisory authority in the relevant jurisdiction(s) (e.g. ICO for UK, relevant EU DPA for EEA)
- **Content:** Nature of breach; categories and approximate number of data subjects and records; likely consequences; measures taken or proposed; contact point for more information

### 2.2 Data Subjects (GDPR Art. 34)

- **When:** Without undue delay if the breach is likely to result in a high risk to rights and freedoms
- **Content:** Nature of breach; contact point for more information; measures taken; advice on mitigating risk

### 2.3 Exceptions

- Notification may not be required if measures (e.g. encryption) make the data unintelligible to unauthorized parties
- Supervisory authority may instruct that data subjects need not be notified if it would involve disproportionate effort (e.g. public communication instead)

---

## 3. Process

1. **Assess:** Determine scope, impact, and risk level
2. **Document:** Record breach details, timeline, and actions
3. **Notify regulator:** Within 72 hours (GDPR)
4. **Notify data subjects:** If high risk; without undue delay
5. **Document:** Retain records of breach and notifications

---

## 4. Notification Channels

- **Users:** In-app notification, email, or both. We will use clear and plain language.
- **Regulators:** Via designated portal or contact (e.g. ICO breach reporting, EU DPA online forms)
- **US state laws:** Many US states require notification to state attorneys general and/or credit reporting agencies when certain thresholds are met. Legal will determine applicable requirements.
- **Public:** If required by law (e.g. certain US states) or where it would be disproportionate to notify each individual
